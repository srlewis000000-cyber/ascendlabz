Frequently Asked Questions

What is GDF-8 (Myostatin) used for in research?

GDF-8, commonly known as myostatin, is a negative regulator of skeletal muscle mass. Research uses include studies of muscle atrophy signaling, body composition regulation, muscle hypertrophy pathways, and the development of myostatin inhibition strategies for muscle wasting conditions.

How does myostatin research relate to muscle growth?

Myostatin acts as a brake on muscle development — it signals satellite cells to remain quiescent and limits muscle fiber hypertrophy. Laboratory research on GDF-8 explores how inhibiting or modulating this protein affects downstream muscle growth regulators including ActRIIB and Smad2/3 pathways.
