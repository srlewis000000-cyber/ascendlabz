Frequently Asked Questions

What is Epitalon and what is it used for in research?

Epitalon (also known as Epithalon or Epithalone) is a synthetic tetrapeptide (Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly) originally derived from the pineal peptide preparation Epithalamin. Research has focused on its ability to activate telomerase expression, influence circadian rhythms, and modulate various aspects of cellular aging and epigenetic regulation.

How does Epitalon relate to telomerase research?

In laboratory studies, Epitalon has been shown to upregulate telomerase activity in somatic cells. Telomerase is the enzyme responsible for maintaining telomere length during cell division, making Epitalon a useful research tool for studying cellular aging, genomic stability, and replicative senescence.
