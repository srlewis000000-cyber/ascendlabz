Frequently Asked Questions

What is HCG used for in research?

HCG is a glycoprotein hormone that mimics luteinizing hormone (LH) through shared receptor binding. Research uses include studies of Leydig cell steroidogenesis, testosterone biosynthesis pathways, gonadotropin receptor signaling, and male reproductive endocrinology in laboratory settings.

How does HCG stimulate testosterone production in research models?

HCG binds to LH/hCG receptors on testicular Leydig cells, activating the cAMP/PKA signaling cascade that stimulates StAR protein expression and the steroidogenic enzyme pathway (CYP11A1, CYP17A1, HSD17B3), ultimately converting cholesterol to testosterone in research models.
