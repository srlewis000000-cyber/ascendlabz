Frequently Asked Questions

What is Melanotan 1 used for in research?

Melanotan 1 (Afamelanotide) is a linear alpha-MSH peptide studied for its activation of melanocortin receptors, particularly MC1R. Research focuses on melanogenesis (melanin production), photoprotection mechanisms, and pigmentation biology in dermatological and photobiology laboratory studies.

How does Melanotan 1 differ from Melanotan 2?

Melanotan 1 is a linear alpha-MSH analog with high MC1R selectivity, while Melanotan 2 is a cyclic analog with broader melanocortin receptor activity (MC1R, MC3R, MC4R, MC5R). MT-1 research focuses primarily on skin pigmentation and photoprotection, while MT-2 research also covers sexual function and appetite pathways.
