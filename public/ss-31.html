Frequently Asked Questions

What is SS-31 (Elamipretide) used for in research?

SS-31 is a mitochondria-targeted tetrapeptide that selectively binds cardiolipin in the inner mitochondrial membrane. Research focuses on its effects on mitochondrial cristae structure, electron transport chain efficiency, ATP production, and reactive oxygen species (ROS) reduction in cell and animal models.

How does SS-31 target mitochondria specifically?

SS-31 contains alternating aromatic and cationic residues that create an amphipathic structure. This allows cell-permeable uptake and selective concentration at the inner mitochondrial membrane through electrostatic interactions with cardiolipin, without requiring a mitochondrial targeting sequence.
