Frequently Asked Questions

What is GHRP-6 used for in research?

GHRP-6 is studied for its ability to stimulate endogenous growth hormone secretion through ghrelin receptor activation. Research explores its roles in GH secretion protocols, appetite pathway studies, and recovery mechanism investigations in laboratory settings.

How does GHRP-6 differ from GHRP-2?

While both are GH-releasing peptides, GHRP-6 exhibits stronger appetite-stimulating properties due to its interaction with ghrelin receptors, making it useful for appetite and metabolic research. GHRP-2 is generally studied for more selective GH secretagogue effects.
