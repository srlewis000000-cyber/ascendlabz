What makes Hexarelin one of the most potent GHRPs for research?

Hexarelin is noted in research for its high potency as a GHS-R1a agonist relative to other GHRPs. Studies indicate it produces a strong and consistent growth hormone secretion response in research models, along with additional cardiovascular-related research applications via CD36 receptor interactions.

How does Hexarelin compare to GHRP-2 and Ipamorelin?

Hexarelin is generally considered more potent than GHRP-2 and Ipamorelin in terms of GH-releasing capacity per microgram. However, it also has broader receptor activity including CD36, making it useful for research into cardiac and metabolic functions beyond GH axis studies.
