Frequently Asked Questions

What is MOTS-c and why is it significant in research?

MOTS-c is a 16-amino acid peptide encoded within the mitochondrial 12S rRNA gene — making it one of the few known peptides of mitochondrial origin. Research has shown it plays roles in metabolic homeostasis, insulin sensitivity, and mitochondria-to-nucleus retrograde signaling, representing a novel class of signaling molecules with broad research implications.

What metabolic pathways does MOTS-c research focus on?

Laboratory research on MOTS-c has primarily focused on its activation of the AMPK pathway, its effects on glucose metabolism and insulin sensitivity in various tissue models, and its role as a mitochondrial stress-responsive signaling molecule. It is also being studied for its potential roles in aging and exercise physiology research.
