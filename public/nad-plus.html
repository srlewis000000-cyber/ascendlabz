What is the difference between NAD+ and NMN or NR?

NAD+ is the active coenzyme form. NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide) and NR (Nicotinamide Riboside) are NAD+ precursors — compounds that the body can convert into NAD+. Researchers study all three, often in comparison studies.
