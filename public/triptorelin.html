Frequently Asked Questions

What is Triptorelin used for in research?

Triptorelin is a synthetic GnRH agonist ([D-Trp6]-GnRH) used in laboratory research to study the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal (HPG) axis. Research applications include investigating GnRH receptor activation, LH/FSH desensitization mechanisms, gonadal steroid regulation, and neuroendocrine feedback pathway studies.

How does Triptorelin act as a GnRH agonist?

Triptorelin binds GnRH receptors on pituitary gonadotrophs with higher affinity and longer receptor occupancy than endogenous GnRH. Initially it stimulates LH/FSH release, but with continuous exposure it causes receptor downregulation and desensitization — making it valuable for studying both acute and chronic HPG axis regulatory mechanisms.
