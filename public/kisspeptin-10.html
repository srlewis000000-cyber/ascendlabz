Frequently Asked Questions

What is Kisspeptin-10 used for in research?

Kisspeptin-10 is the C-terminal decapeptide fragment of the KISS1 gene product. Research focuses on its role as a GPR54 agonist in the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis, including GnRH pulse regulation, LH/FSH secretion stimulation, and reproductive neuroendocrinology studies.

How does Kisspeptin-10 stimulate reproductive hormone release?

Kisspeptin-10 activates GPR54 receptors on hypothalamic GnRH neurons, triggering pulsatile GnRH release. This downstream stimulates pituitary LH and FSH secretion, making Kisspeptin-10 a key research tool for studying the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis in laboratory settings.
